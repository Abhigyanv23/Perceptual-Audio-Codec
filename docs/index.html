a codec that decides what you can't hear

Most of every song
is inaudible.

This is a lossy audio codec built from first principles — the same core idea behind MP3 and AAC. It transforms your audio into frequency bands, models which of those bands your ear can't actually perceive because a louder neighbor is masking them, and spends its bit budget only on what remains. how it actually works

Five steps, in order

  1. 01

    MDCT

    Audio is split into overlapping windows and converted from a waveform into frequency lines using the Modified Discrete Cosine Transform — the same transform at the heart of MP3, AAC, and Vorbis.

  2. 02

    Bark-scale masking model

    Frequency lines are grouped into critical bands spaced the way your cochlea actually resolves pitch. A loud band spreads a "masking shadow" onto its neighbors — sounds under that shadow become inaudible.

  3. 03

    Threshold of hearing

    Even with no masking at all, extremely quiet or extremely high/low frequencies are floored against the absolute threshold of human hearing — no point spending bits below what anyone could ever hear.

  4. 04

    Greedy bit allocation

    Bits are the scarce resource. Each one buys about 6 dB of extra precision. The allocator keeps handing the next bit to whichever band is currently the most audible above its masking threshold.

  5. 05

    Quantize & reconstruct

    Each band is rounded to its allotted precision — coarse where masking hides it, fine where your ear would catch it — then the inverse transform overlaps everything back into audio.

what this is (and isn't)

This demo runs the full DSP pipeline for real, in your browser, on your audio — nothing here is precomputed or faked. For speed inside a browser tab it processes the first 15 seconds of whatever you drop in, and it skips a few things production codecs add: entropy coding on top of the quantized values, block-size switching for transients, and per-band tonality analysis. The transform and the masking model — the perceptually interesting parts — are real. The full Python version in this repo includes a real bitstream format you can actually decode a file from.

Perceptual-Audio-Codec MDCT · Bark-scale masking · greedy bit allocation — all running client-side, no server involved.
